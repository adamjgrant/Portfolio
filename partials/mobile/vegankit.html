The Vision

First and foremost, VeganKit needed to be the starter kit on veganism and nothing else--not a splash page to another site, not a tab on some recipe page, but a standalone and dedicated gem of the life of veganism.

An example of social proofing used throughout the site.
A couple of "fan mentions" of VeganKit we've spotted over the years.

To accomplish this, we started with the user, iterating over personas we created from the many encounters we've had over the years with people interested in going vegan. We user-tested and card sorted with different categories, trying to keep these limited to about 5-6 and came up with the navigation we have today.

The goal was to ensure consistent flow through the pages regardless of the user's unique motivation--environmental concerns, animal ethics, diet, etc. We set out to create highly relevant categories filled with non-academic language, warm and faded colors, high quality custom photos with a DSLR camera, and social proof via YouTube videos and saved tweets.

The site was initially built in static HTML, then rewritten in PHP, and finally rewritten again in Django to employ international translations. Thanks to a swarm of our international friends, this is currently in progress.
